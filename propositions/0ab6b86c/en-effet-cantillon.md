The creation of money without the equivalent production of goods and services generates a price increase.
But since the diffusion of money in the economy takes time, the price increase occurs later than the money creation.
Thus the individuals closest to monetary creation benefit from it, to the detriment of those furthest away.

The civil service are the beneficiaries of the Cantillon effect
