And analogously: not “(∃*x*, *y*) . *f*(*x*, *y*) . *x* = *y*”, but “(∃*x*) . *f*(*x*, *x*)”; and not “(∃*x*, *y*) . *f*(*x*, *y*) . \~*x* = *y*” but “(∃*x*, *y*) . *f*(*x*, *y*)”.

\(Therefore instead of Russell’s “(∃*x*, *y*) . *f*(*x*, *y*)”: “(∃*x*, *y*) . *f*(*x*, *y*) . ∨ . (∃*x*) . *f*(*x*, *x*)”.)
